Research Focus

My research focuses specifically on Fine-grained Visual Categorization, which aims to distinguish subordinate-level categories like bird species. For many years, computer vision has focused on recognizing basic-level categories. Psychologists have showed that humans do well in basic-level recognition before developing their ability of subordinate-level recognition. Perhaps not coincidentally, researches in the field of object recognition have followed a twin trajectory, moving from coarse-grained to fine-grained. In contrast to basic-level categorization, subordinate-level classification needs to explicitly discriminate against subtle differences among similar subcategories.

Fine-grained categorization is a fundamental part of object recognition. Progress in fine-grained categorization not only expands the scope of generic object recognition, but also directly benefits domain experts.
